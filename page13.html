Introduction to Stars

Stars are luminous celestial bodies made of plasma held together by their own gravity. These brilliant objects have been a source of fascination for humanity for centuries, guiding travelers, inspiring myths, and playing a vital role in understanding the cosmos.

Types of Stars

Protostars

Protostars are the earliest stage in a star's lifecycle, formed from collapsing clouds of gas and dust.

Main Sequence Stars

The majority of stars in the universe are main sequence stars, burning hydrogen into helium at their cores.

Red Giants

These massive stars expand and cool as they exhaust their hydrogen fuel, appearing red in color.

Famous Stars in the Universe

  • Polaris: Known as the North Star, it has been used for navigation for centuries.
  • Betelgeuse: A red supergiant in the Orion constellation, it is nearing the end of its lifecycle.
  • Sirius: The brightest star in Earth's night sky, located in the Canis Major constellation.
