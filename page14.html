Introduction to Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems composed of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter, bound together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, from dwarf galaxies with a few billion stars to giants with one hundred trillion stars or more.

Types of Galaxies

Spiral Galaxies

Characterized by their rotating spiral arms, these galaxies are rich in gas and dust, often sites of active star formation.

Elliptical Galaxies

These galaxies range from nearly spherical to highly elongated shapes, containing older, lower-mass stars, with less gas and dust.

Irregular Galaxies

Lacking a defined shape, irregular galaxies often result from gravitational interactions or collisions with other galaxies.

Famous Galaxies in the Universe

  • Milky Way: Our home galaxy, a barred spiral galaxy containing our Solar System.
  • Andromeda: The closest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way, on a collision course with our galaxy.
  • Messier 87 (M87): An enormous elliptical galaxy with a supermassive black hole at its center, known for its jet of energetic particles.
